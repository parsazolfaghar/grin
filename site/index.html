[ 01 / WHAT IT IS ]

An operator that doesn't wait for you.

GRIN is an autonomous offensive-security orchestrator. Give it an authorized target and a goal; it plans the engagement, drives Kali & BlackArch tooling, gets a foothold, escalates, pivots across hosts, and captures the proof — then writes it up. Every action passes through a single fail-closed spine that enforces scope and logs an audit trail, so it cannot wander off the targets you authorized.

[ 02 / CAPABILITIES ]

Why it actually lands.

D-01

Deterministic closers

When the model would stall, code takes the last mile through the spine: default-cred sweep, command-injection, sudo & SUID privesc, LFI→crack, SQLi, and SSH-key lateral movement. The win isn't left to luck.

D-02

The Grin Brain

Persistent, cross-engagement memory. It recognizes the situation and applies the proven play every time — and learns from every success and wall.

D-03

Dual arsenal

Self-provisioning Kali + BlackArch containers, complementary by design — tools route across both. A laptop with Docker is a full rig.

Every action: resolve → authorize → gate → execute → audit. Out-of-scope is refused, not run. The model can't go around it.
